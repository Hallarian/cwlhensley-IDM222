The Art of Digital Media

Not everyone knows what they want to be when they grow up. With me, I didn’t know that I knew it. Or perhaps I was just waiting for technology catch up to my imagination. I have always loved television, movies, games, and have been fascinated with advances in technology that help bring these genres together. This is what interests me so much about digital media.

Digital media can be defined as any media that is encoded into a machine-readable format. It can be created, viewed, distributed, modified and preserved on any electronic device that supports it.

And we all know how increasingly important digital media is becoming in our lives. When I think about how far we have come since the commercialization of the Internet 20 year ago, I am intrigued by where we’ll be 20 years from now. We live in a world where the lines between information and entertainment are being blurred, and in many cases disappearing altogether.

Digital media allows us to be much more than consumers; it allows us to be producers, content creators. If you have access to a computers and the Internet there is a plethora of opportunities to connect, engage and create media and foster relationships in the process. There is much fun to be had in participating in the word of digital media.

I myself have a creative aptitude and passion for this that I am constantly looking for ways to improve my depth of knowledge, and in turn share what I have learned with others. I acquired some skill sets in this field during my time in the Navy. Typically, I find great interest and motivation using computer programs and software; such as Photoshop, InDesign and Illustrator; these and other digital media tools open up so many possibilities for creativity and self-discovery.

This poster was created in photoshop by me; here I was experimenting with settings in the program to help create an otherworldly feel. The idea for the poster itself is based off an story I have been writng over the years. you can find some sample chapters in Communication Studies tab under Fiction. The image was also taken by me.
