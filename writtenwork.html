The Art of Writing


In the better part of my 35 year existence, I have looked for meaning in my life. And in that time I have found one thing to be true; life is a series of communication threads that form the web of our reality; and semiotics dominates that existence. Always, I have listen to someone tell me about their life experiences, and always have I studied the signs.

I personally used this strategy on numerous occasions, during crucial times in my life when I need examples of change to help me take control over some aspect of my life. This consistently resulted in preventing myself from making a mistake or rectifying one I already made.

“Meaning is modiﬁed through an interpretive process.” In order to survive I had to extract meaning from every encounter, every word and every action of others, then transform it. For years, I have lived by the creed of ‘learn from others’ mistakes and experiences’. This is who I am, this is my ‘self-concept’; it is my ability to see who I was, who I am, and who I could be, by extracting the meaning of life’s symbols and internalizing it.

Why do I say all this? Well, because the above statement in an excerpt from a paper I wrote called “My Self-Assessment, My Self Concept”. This just one example of several essays I have written in the field of Communication Studies. Also contained within this section are other fiction and nonfiction writings. Writing has always flowed very naturally for me. When I write it is always a very therapeutic and personal experience for me. You may say I am putting too much of myself out there, but I that is how I write, and more importantly who I am. Enjoy.
